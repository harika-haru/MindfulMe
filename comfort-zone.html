Anxiety

Anxiety is a mental health condition characterized by excessive worry, fear, and tension. It can affect daily life and well-being.

ADHD

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder that affects both children and adults.

Anorexia nervosa

The symptoms and causes of anorexia, what help is available and ways you can look after yourself.

Bipolar

This involves at least one manic episode, which may be followed by a major depressive episode.

Body Image

Body image refers to a person's perceptions, thoughts, and feelings about their physical appearance.

Bulimia nervosa

Bulimia nervosa, often known as bulimia, involves episodes of binge eating followed by compensatory behaviors like vomiting, exercise, or laxatives.

CBT

TCognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a psychotherapy approach that focuses on helping individuals identify and change unhelpful thought patterns and behaviors.

Crisis care

Crisis care refers to immediate and short-term intervention and support provided to individuals who are experiencing acute psychological, emotional, or situational distress.

Bulimia nervosa

Bulimia nervosa, often known as bulimia, involves episodes of binge eating followed by compensatory behaviors like vomiting, exercise, or laxatives.

D - Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest in activities.

Dysthymia

Dysthymia is a chronic form of depression that involves long-term, low-level symptoms of depression that last for at least two years.

Delusional Disorder

Delusional disorder is characterized by the presence of one or more false beliefs (delusions) that persist for at least one month. These beliefs are often unrealistic and unrelated to cultural or religious beliefs.

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are a range of psychological conditions that cause unhealthy eating habits.

Elderly Depression

Depression in the elderly is a common mental health issue, often caused by isolation and medical conditions.

Emotional Resilience

Emotional resilience is the ability to adapt to and cope with challenging life situations.

Fibromyalgia

Fibromyalgia is a chronic pain condition characterized by tender points, soft tissue swelling, and muscle stiffness.

Gender Dysphoria

Gender dysphoria is a condition where a person experiences significant distress due to the incongruence between their experienced gender and their assigned gender at birth.

Generalized Anxiety Disorder

Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) is characterized by excessive, uncontrollable worry and anxiety about everyday life events.

Grief and Bereavement

Grief and bereavement are natural responses to the loss of a loved one and can lead to feelings of sadness and mourning.

Hoarding Disorder

Hoarding disorder is characterized by persistent difficulty in parting with possessions, leading to cluttered living spaces and distress.

Hypochondria

Hypochondria, also known as illness anxiety disorder, involves excessive worry about having a serious illness, even when there's no medical evidence of it.

Intermittent Explosive Disorder

Intermittent explosive disorder is a mental health condition characterized by sudden, aggressive, and impulsive outbursts of anger or violence.

Insomnia

Insomnia is a common sleep disorder that involves difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, leading to sleep deprivation.

Loneliness

Loneliness is a common human emotion but can lead to negative mental health effects when experienced frequently or intensely.

Learning Disabilities

Learning disabilities are neurological disorders that can affect one's ability to acquire, store, process, and use information.

LGBTQ+ Mental Health

LGBTQ+ individuals may face unique mental health challenges related to discrimination, stigma, and identity-related stressors.

Jealousy

Jealousy is a complex emotion that can impact mental health, relationships, and self-esteem when not managed effectively.

Joy

Joy is a positive emotion that contributes to mental well-being and is essential for a fulfilling life.

Job Stress

Job-related stress can affect mental health, leading to burnout and other issues. Coping strategies are important.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD is a mental health disorder characterized by repetitive, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and behaviors (compulsions).

Overthinking

Overthinking is a habit that can lead to anxiety and stress, affecting mental health. Techniques to reduce overthinking are important.

Optimism

Optimism is a positive outlook on life that can contribute to better mental health and well-being.

Panic Attacks

Panic attacks are sudden and intense bouts of fear and anxiety, impacting mental health and daily life.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD can develop following a traumatic event, affecting mental health. Treatment and support are essential for recovery.

Positive Psychology

Positive psychology is the study of well-being and the promotion of factors that contribute to mental health and happiness.

Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)

Major depressive disorder, commonly known as depression, is a mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest in daily activities.

Mood Disorders

Mood disorders include a range of conditions, such as bipolar disorder and depressive disorders, characterized by disturbances in mood and emotional states.

Multiple Personality Disorder (Dissociative Identity Disorder)

Multiple personality disorder, now known as dissociative identity disorder (DID), involves the presence of two or more distinct identity states.

Narcissistic Personality Disorder

Narcissistic personality disorder involves a pervasive pattern of grandiosity, a need for admiration, and a lack of empathy for others.

Social Anxiety Disorder

Social anxiety disorder, also known as social phobia, is characterized by an intense fear of social situations and a fear of being negatively judged by others.

Somatization Disorder

Somatization disorder involves the expression of psychological distress through physical symptoms and complaints, often leading to numerous doctor visits and tests.

Tourette's Syndrome

Tourette's syndrome is a neurological condition characterized by repetitive, involuntary movements and vocalizations called tics.

Unipolar Depression

Unipolar depression is another term for major depressive disorder and is characterized by recurrent episodes of depression without mania.

Understanding Mental Health

Understanding mental health is essential for promoting well-being, reducing stigma, and supporting those with mental health conditions.

Urological Disorders

Urological disorders encompass conditions that affect the urinary system and male reproductive organs, often linked to mental health issues.

Victim Mentality

Victim mentality is a mindset in which an individual sees themselves as a constant victim, which can impact mental health and relationships.

Virtual Reality Therapy

Virtual reality therapy is an innovative approach to mental health treatment, using VR technology to address various conditions and phobias.

Violent Behavior

Violent behavior may be related to various mental health disorders, and understanding its causes and prevention is crucial.

Wellness

Wellness is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being, and it encompasses lifestyle choices and mental health practices.

Work-Life Balance

Work-life balance is the equilibrium between work-related activities and personal life, crucial for mental health and overall well-being.

Worry

Excessive worry can be a symptom of generalized anxiety disorder and negatively impact mental health and daily functioning.

Xenophobia

Xenophobia is the fear or hatred of people from other countries or backgrounds and can lead to social and mental health issues.

Xerophthalmia

Xerophthalmia is a medical condition related to severe dryness of the eye's surface, which can impact an individual's quality of life.

Xylophobia

Xylophobia is an irrational and persistent fear of wood or wooden objects and may be linked to specific phobias and mental health.

Youth Mental Health

Youth mental health is a critical area of focus, as young people may experience unique challenges and stressors that impact their well-being.

Yoga and Mental Health

Yoga is a mind-body practice that can have a positive impact on mental health, promoting relaxation and stress reduction.

Yearning

Yearning or longing for something or someone can affect an individual's emotional and mental state, often associated with attachment.

Zoophobia

Zoophobia is an irrational fear of animals or specific creatures, and addressing such phobias can improve mental health.

Zen Meditation

Zen meditation is a form of meditation that emphasizes self-realization and can have a calming effect on mental well-being.

Zoonotic Diseases

Zoonotic diseases are infections that can be transmitted from animals to humans, emphasizing the importance of public health and mental well-being.
